--- MUD PUMP MANUAL ---
Device: Triplex Mud Pump
Model: ONGC-MP-1600
Manufacturer: ONGC Workshop
Equipment Class: rotating / reciprocating
Document: ONGC-MP-1600 Triplex Mud Pump O&M Manual, rev3

Specifications:
- Type: Triplex single-acting reciprocating piston pump
- Rated power: 1600 HP at 120 strokes per minute
- Drive: AC motor through reduction gearbox, chain final drive
- Fluid end: forged steel, replaceable liners 5" to 7"
- Lubrication: splash-fed power end with pressurized auxiliary loop

Section 4.2 — Operating Limits:
- Fluid end temperature, continuous operation: 20C to 55C. Alarm at 60C. Emergency shutdown (trip) at 75C.
- Structural vibration, continuous operation: 0 to 3.0 g RMS. Alarm at 5.5 g RMS. Trip at 9.0 g RMS.
- Operation above alarm levels is permitted only for controlled shutdown, never for continued drilling.

Section 4.3 — Diagnostics & Failure Modes:
1. Main Bearing Seizure: If fluid end temperature exceeds the 60C alarm level AND vibration rises above 5.5 g RMS simultaneously, the main crankshaft bearing is running dry and seizure is imminent. Continued operation past the 75C trip point will crack the power-end housing. Listen for a low-frequency rumble that rises with stroke rate.
- Required Action: 'Replace Main Bearing Assembly'. Lock out and tag out the drive motor, drain the power-end oil and inspect it for bronze flakes, and inspect the crankshaft journal for scoring before re-assembly.
2. Liner Wash (Piston/Liner Wear): A worn liner lets drilling mud cut past the piston. The signature is rising vibration (above 5.5 g RMS) with broadly normal fluid end temperature (below 60C), often accompanied by a rhythmic knocking synchronized to the stroke rate and a measurable drop in discharge volume.
- Required Action: 'Replace Liner and Piston'. Verify rod alignment and re-torque the liner clamp to specification after replacement.
3. Packing Gland Failure: Worn stuffing-box packing leaks high-pressure mud past the rod. The signature is a sharp hissing/spraying noise rising with pump speed, together with elevated vibration as the rod loses support. Fluid end temperature usually remains normal.
- Required Action: 'Repack Stuffing Box'. Replace all packing rings as a set, inspect the rod surface for grooving, and re-tighten the gland nut to the running torque in Table 9.

Section 6 — Maintenance Schedule:
- Every shift: check power-end oil level, listen for knocking, check packing leak rate.
- Weekly: grease the crosshead, verify the vibration baseline is under 3.0 g RMS.
- Monthly: pull and inspect one liner, calibrate the fluid end temperature probe.

--- CONTROL PANEL MANUAL ---
Device: Rig Control Panel
Model: VoltGuard CP-440
Manufacturer: VoltGuard
Equipment Class: static / electrical-hydraulic
Document: VoltGuard CP-440 Control Panel Manual, rev1

Specifications:
- Supply: 440 V three-phase, 60 A
- Hydraulic section: 22 bar rated control circuit with integral relief valve
- Enclosure: IP65, forced ventilation with replaceable dust filter
- Controls: motor starters, BOP control solenoids, emergency stop chain

Section 3.1 — Operating Limits:
- Hydraulic control line pressure, normal operation: 5 to 16 bar. High alarm at 18 bar. High trip (relief) at 22 bar. Low alarm at 4 bar. Low-low trip at 2 bar.
- A low control-line pressure is as dangerous as a high one: below the 4 bar low alarm the BOP control solenoids cannot develop enough force to actuate, and below 2 bar the panel can no longer guarantee closure of the well-control stack.
- Internal panel air temperature must remain within the ambient room limits; the panel has no independent temperature transmitter.

Section 3.4 — Diagnostics & Failure Modes:
1. Hydraulic Line Blockage: A blocked or kinked control line causes pressure to climb steadily above the 18 bar alarm point while actuator response becomes sluggish. Pressure trapped above 22 bar risks bursting the line.
- Required Action: 'Flush Hydraulic Control Line'. Depressurize via the bleed port first, then back-flush the line and replace the inline filter element.
2. Relief Valve Failure: If line pressure exceeds 18 bar and does NOT relieve at the rated 22 bar set point, the relief valve has seized. This is a containment-critical condition.
- Required Action: 'Replace Relief Valve'. Isolate the HPU, verify zero trapped pressure, replace the valve cartridge, and re-certify the set point with a calibrated dead-weight tester.
3. Panel Overheating (Ventilation Blockage): A clogged dust filter or failed ventilation fan lets the room-side temperature climb around the panel. Sustained ambient temperature above the area warning level near the panel degrades contactor life and can nuisance-trip the starters.
- Required Action: 'Clear Panel Ventilation and Replace Filter'. De-energize the panel, replace the filter mat, verify fan rotation, and thermal-scan the busbars before returning to service.
4. Hydraulic Pressure Loss: Control line pressure falling BELOW the 4 bar low alarm — and especially below the 2 bar low-low trip — means the hydraulic power unit is no longer maintaining the control circuit, from a pump failure, a downstream leak, or an open bleed/test port left cracked. With low control pressure the BOP and motor-control solenoids will not hold, so this is a well-control-critical condition, not a nuisance.
- Required Action: 'Inspect HPU and Restore Hydraulic Supply'. Confirm the HPU pump is running and the accumulator is charged, walk the control lines for leaks, close any open bleed/test ports, and re-pressurize to the 5-16 bar normal band before relying on the panel for well control.

Section 5 — Maintenance Schedule:
- Monthly: replace ventilation filter mat, exercise the emergency stop chain.
- Quarterly: verify relief valve set point, torque-check power terminations.

--- COMPRESSOR MANUAL ---
Device: Rig Air Compressor
Model: ACME-COMP-2200
Manufacturer: ACME
Equipment Class: rotating
Document: ACME-COMP-2200 Rig Air Compressor Manual, rev2

Specifications:
- Type: two-stage reciprocating air compressor
- Rated delivery: 2200 L/min FAD at 16 bar
- Drive: direct-coupled 75 kW induction motor
- Cooling: air-cooled, finned intercooler and aftercooler

Section 5.4 — Operating Limits:
- Casing temperature, continuous operation: 20C to 58C. Alarm at 58C. Trip at 72C.
- Frame vibration: 0 to 2.8 g RMS. Alarm at 4.5 g RMS. Trip at 7.0 g RMS.
- Discharge pressure, rated: 6 to 16 bar. High alarm at 19 bar. High trip at 24 bar. Low alarm at 5 bar. Low trip at 3 bar.

Section 5.6 — Diagnostics & Failure Modes:
1. Motor Burnout: If casing temperature exceeds the 58C alarm AND frame vibration exceeds 4.5 g RMS, the drive motor windings are overheating under mechanical load. Past the 72C trip point the winding insulation fails permanently and the rotor may seize.
- Required Action: 'Replace Motor'. Do not attempt a rewind; the rotor is likely warped. Megger-test the replacement before coupling and verify alignment to within 0.05 mm.
2. Discharge Valve Failure: Discharge pressure climbing above the 19 bar alarm with elevated casing temperature (above 58C) but near-normal vibration indicates the discharge valve plates are leaking and re-compressing hot air.
- Required Action: 'Replace Valve Plate Assembly'. Inspect the unloader mechanism and replace the head gasket while the head is off.
3. Bearing Wear: Frame vibration alone climbing past 4.5 g RMS, with temperature and discharge pressure inside normal limits, indicates progressive main bearing wear. The vibration spectrum shows a characteristic 2x running-speed component.
- Required Action: 'Replace Bearings'. Re-grease to specification and verify shaft end-float after replacement.
4. Discharge Pressure Loss: Discharge pressure falling BELOW the 5 bar low alarm (and toward the 3 bar low trip) while the motor runs normally indicates the compressor is failing to build pressure — typically a clogged intake filter, an unloader valve stuck open, or a downstream leak. Casing temperature and vibration usually stay near normal, which distinguishes this from a mechanical failure.
- Required Action: 'Inspect Intake and Unloader'. Check and clear the intake filter, verify the unloader/pilot is seating, and walk the discharge line for leaks before reloading the machine.

Section 7 — Maintenance Schedule:
- Daily: drain receiver condensate, check oil level.
- Every 500 hours: replace air filter, sample oil, record vibration baseline.
- Every 2000 hours: replace valve plates, inspect intercooler for fouling.

--- WELLHEAD MANUAL ---
Device: Wellhead Assembly
Model: WH-5000 Series
Manufacturer: WH Industries
Equipment Class: static / pressure-containing
Document: WH-5000 Series Wellhead Assembly Manual, rev4

Specifications:
- Rated working pressure: 5000 psi
- Configuration: casing head, tubing head, christmas tree with master valve, wing valve, adjustable choke
- Seals: annular elastomer seals, H2S (sour) service rated per NACE MR0175

Section 7.2 — Operating Limits:
- H2S concentration at the wellhead: 0 to 5 ppm normal. Alarm at 8 ppm. Evacuation / emergency shutdown at 12 ppm. (Stricter than the general area standard because the wellhead is the most probable leak source.)
- Acoustic noise at one meter: 40 to 88 dB normal. Alarm at 92 dB. Critical at 102 dB.

Section 7.5 — Diagnostics & Failure Modes:
1. Annular Seal Leak: ANY sustained H2S reading above the 8 ppm alarm at the wellhead indicates the annular seal is passing formation gas. This is a life-safety event. Above 12 ppm the area must be evacuated immediately.
- Required Action: 'Activate Emergency Shutdown and Replace Annular Seal'. Evacuate the zone, close the master valve, and re-enter only with SCBA until H2S returns to 0 ppm. Replace the seal as a complete energized-seal kit.
2. Choke Erosion: A rising high-pitched flow noise above the 92 dB alarm point, without an accompanying gas reading, indicates the choke bean is eroding and flow is cutting the body.
- Required Action: 'Replace Choke Bean'. Inspect the choke body for wash-out and verify downstream pipework wall thickness ultrasonically.
3. Master Valve Passing: A continuous low-frequency flow noise above the 92 dB alarm with a simultaneous trace H2S reading (above 8 ppm) downstream of a closed master valve indicates the gate is not sealing and gas is passing the valve.
- Required Action: 'Lap or Replace Master Valve Gate'. Confirm with a downstream pressure build-up test, then service the gate and seats; replace the valve if seat pockets are washed.

Section 9 — Maintenance Schedule:
- Daily: walk-down for hissing, bubbling, or paint blistering at flanges.
- Monthly: function-test master and wing valves, bump-test the H2S detector.
- Annually: full pressure test to rated working pressure, seal kit inspection.

--- ZONE ENVIRONMENTAL SAFETY STANDARD ---
Document: ONGC HSE Field Standard HSE-2025-04 (Occupied Work Areas)
Scope: Applies to ambient, room-level sensors in any occupied zone (work rooms and corridors). These limits protect personnel, not specific equipment. Device-mounted sensors must use their device manual limits instead.

Section 2 — Area Limits:
- Ambient air temperature: 18C to 45C normal. Warning at 48C. Critical at 60C (heat-stress evacuation).
- H2S concentration, general area: 0 to 5 ppm normal. Warning at 10 ppm. Critical at 15 ppm (mandatory evacuation, OSHA ceiling).
- Continuous noise exposure: 40 to 85 dB normal. Warning at 95 dB. Critical at 105 dB (double hearing protection plus time limits).
- Service line pressure in occupied areas: 5 to 15 bar normal. High warning at 20 bar. High critical at 25 bar. Low warning at 4 bar. Low critical at 2 bar (loss of service supply).
- Structural vibration in occupied areas: 0 to 2.5 g RMS normal. Warning at 5.0 g RMS. Critical at 8.0 g RMS.

Section 3 — Area Hazard Responses:
1. Area H2S Gas Accumulation: Any area H2S warning (above 10 ppm) means formation gas is accumulating in the occupied space, from a leak or from poor ventilation. Above 15 ppm evacuation is mandatory.
- Required Action: 'Evacuate Zone and Ventilate'. Account for all personnel, start forced ventilation, cross-check with a portable detector, and identify the leak source before re-entry.
2. Heat Stress Hazard: Sustained ambient temperature above the 48C warning level creates a heat-stress risk and usually indicates an equipment heat source or failed cooling in the zone.
- Required Action: 'Remove Heat Source and Ventilate'. Limit occupancy time, identify the radiating equipment, and restore cooling or ventilation before normal work resumes.
3. Excessive Area Noise: Continuous noise above the 95 dB warning level exceeds safe daily exposure even with single hearing protection.
- Required Action: 'Enforce Hearing Protection and Locate Noise Source'. Mandate double protection above 105 dB, limit exposure time, and inspect rotating equipment in the zone for the source.
4. Structural Vibration Hazard: Area vibration above 5.0 g RMS indicates a machine transmitting abnormal vibration into the structure; above 8.0 g RMS there is risk to walkways and supports.
- Required Action: 'Stop Equipment and Inspect Structure'. Identify and stop the offending machine, then inspect anchor bolts, skid welds, and grating before restart.
5. Service Line Overpressure: Area service line pressure above the 20 bar warning level exceeds the rating of utility hoses and quick-connects in occupied areas.
- Required Action: 'Isolate and Depressurize Service Line'. Close the upstream isolation valve, bleed the line to a safe location, and verify the regulator set point before restoring supply.
6. Service Line Pressure Loss: Area service line pressure falling below the 4 bar low warning (toward the 2 bar low critical) indicates a service-line leak or an upstream supply failure, which can starve safety-critical utilities (purge air, flushing, instrument supply) in the occupied zone.
- Required Action: 'Isolate and Investigate Pressure Loss'. Isolate the affected branch, walk the line for leaks and open drains, confirm the upstream regulator and supply are intact, and restore the 5-15 bar normal band before relying on the service.
